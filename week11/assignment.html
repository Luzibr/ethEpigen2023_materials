The enrichment analysis aims to examine if the genes that exhibit differential methylation between cancer and normal samples have specific functions that occur more frequently on the 22nd chromosome than would be expected by chance.

The findings range from broad and uninformative in the case of the GO Biological process, which suggests that these genes are involved in cellular regulation and signaling. On the other hand, the GO molecular function provides more detailed insights by indicating that these genes bind cation co-factors and are enriched for kinases, which are well-known to play a central role in certain cell cycle pathways, often affected in cancer cells. Finally, the GO Cellular component points towards organelles, although it doesn’t offer much information, it is possible to speculate that it could be related to mitochondria, as they often undergo significant changes in their roles in cancer.
